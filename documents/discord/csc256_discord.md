**Standalone Notes** [various dates]

- CSC 256 (Machine Structures) is taught primarily in MIPS assembly. You will write assembly code, not Java.
- Discrete Mathematics (CSC 230) is a required prerequisite for CSC 256.
- CSC 256 is a prerequisite for CSC 415 (Operating Systems). Failing or doing poorly means retaking it before you can take 415.
- The textbook used in some sections is *Computer Organization (MIPS)* by Patterson and Hennessy, 6th edition (Morgan-Kaufmann).
- Professors who have taught CSC 256 at SFSU: Abdur Sikder, Bao Tran, Jingyi Wang, William Hsu, Robert Bierman, Shubh Pachchigar, Anudeep (surname unclear).

**Professor summaries from student experiences:**

- **Bao Tran**: Strongly and repeatedly warned against by multiple students. Assignments are hard and sometimes contain errors even tutors can't solve. No helpful support when students are stuck. Class is point-based (may deviate from syllabus weights). Multiple students ended up retaking the course or switching to C/NC.
- **Abdur Sikder**: Easy workload — roughly 1 assignment per month, laid back pacing. Exams/quizzes based on homework, homework based on practice problems from class. Has a thick accent that can make lectures hard to follow; some students recommend supplementing with online resources. Moved from online to in-person around Spring 2022. Mixed reviews on teaching quality but general consensus is the class itself is manageable.
- **William Hsu**: Mumbles during lecture (~40% audible). Only 4–5 easy homeworks per semester based on in-class examples. Tests require memorizing definitions word-for-word, including ones only stated verbally in class (not written on slides). Covered very little actual MIPS programming — only 2 simple arithmetic programs, never reached jumps. Grade-wise survivable (one student got a B) but not recommended for learning the material.
- **Jingyi Wang**: Paper midterms, not too hard — multiple choice plus 1–2 coding questions. Final project in one semester was an "ancient robot game." Some students found her class reasonable. Attendance policy uncertain.
- **Robert Bierman**: More rigorous than most sections; students say they struggled but retained more. Some describe it as hard but ultimately worthwhile for preparation in later courses like CSC 415. One student's first-semester online version was described as "stupidly easy" with the professor showing exactly how to complete homework in class.
