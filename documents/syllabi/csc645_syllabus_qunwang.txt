Course Description
Computer networks, ranging from wired Ethernet to wireless cellular networks, have become an essential part of modern human life, which are also the enabling technologies for companies such as Google, Facebook, and Amazon. This course introduces the fundamental principles and methods on design and implementation of computer networks and network protocols. Topics covered in this course include TCP/IP Protocol Stack, Packet Switching, Reliable Data Transfer, Congestion Control, Routing, Multiple Access Control, Internet Protocols (HTTP, SMTP, TCP, UDP, IP, RIP, OSPF, BGP), Socket Programming, and other emerging topics.

 

Learning Outcomes
At the end of this course, students will

Have basic knowledge on how to design, analyze, and implement computer networks
Gain hands-on experience in network programming and network troubleshooting tools

Welcome to CSC 645: Computer Networks!
 

Hello everyone, and welcome!

I'm Qun Wang, and I'll be your instructor for our journey into the world of computer networks this semester.

This isn't your typical theory-heavy networking course. My goal is to demystify the internet and the complex systems that connect us all. We will move beyond abstract concepts and dive into the practical, hands-on skills that power today's digital world. You will learn not just what a protocol is, but how to write code that uses it, how to analyze network traffic, and how to design and build your own network applications.

Our course is built around a hands-on philosophy. Through Python programming labs, practical analysis with tools like Wireshark, and a semester-long final project of your own design, you will gain tangible skills that are directly applicable in the tech industry.

I am passionate about the future of networking, especially the intersection of AI and network systems, which is the focus of my work at the CIDER LabLinks to an external site.. I'm excited to bring this cutting-edge perspective into our classroom.

Let's get ready for a challenging but rewarding semester. I'm here to support you every step of the way.

Practical Tools: The course will utilize Python, Wireshark, and Unix commands for projects.
Key Topics: DNS, ARP, ICMP, HTTPS, and more.
Course Outline
Weeks 1-15: Learning Journey
Application Layer: Starting from the uppermost layer, you’ll understand protocols like HTTP and HTTPS, DNS, and more.
Transport Layer: Delve into TCP/UDP, learning about ports, connections, and data transfer reliability.
Network Layer: Explore IP addressing, routing algorithms, and protocols like ICMP and ARP.
Datalink Layer: Conclude with understanding MAC addresses, ARP, VLANs, and WiFi technologies.
Weekly Structure
Quizzes: Assessing your understanding of the week's material.
Attendance: Participation in class is essential.
Projects: Practical assignments using Python, Wireshark, and Unix commands. These projects will help you apply concepts like DNS, ARP, and ICMP in real-world scenarios.
Final Stages of the Course
Review Sessions: Comprehensive review sessions in Week 16 to consolidate your learning.
Final Project: A thorough project to evaluate your overall understanding of computer networks.
Upon completion of this course, you will have a solid foundation in computer networking principles. You’ll be equipped with the knowledge to understand network functionality and the critical role of various protocols in the seamless operation of networks.

Looking forward to guiding you through this exciting and informative journey into the world of computer networks!
